Optics

Reflection, refraction, lenses, mirrors, diffraction, polarization.

1. Reflection

Light bouncing off a surface. Law of reflection:

\\[ \\theta_i = \\theta_r \\]

Example: Mirror imaging and periscope behavior.

2. Refraction

Light bending through media. Snell's Law:

\\[ n_1 \\sin \\theta_1 = n_2 \\sin \\theta_2 \\]

Example: Light bending in water; lenses focusing light.

3. Lenses and Mirrors

  • Lens formula: \\( \\frac{1}{f} = \\frac{1}{v} - \\frac{1}{u} \\)
  • Mirror formula: \\( \\frac{1}{f} = \\frac{1}{u} + \\frac{1}{v} \\)
  • Magnification: \\( M = \\frac{v}{u} \\)

4. Diffraction & Interference

Wave bending around obstacles; interference patterns create bright & dark fringes:

\\[ d \\sin \\theta = n \\lambda \\]

5. Polarization

Light waves oscillate in a preferred plane using polarizers; applications: glare reduction, photography.
