Kinetic Theory & Gases

Ideal gases, molecular motion, distribution of speeds, and real gases.

1. Basic Assumptions

  • Gases consist of a large number of tiny particles (molecules) in random motion.
  • The volume of molecules is negligible compared to the container volume.
  • No intermolecular forces except during collisions.
  • Collisions are perfectly elastic.

2. Ideal Gas Law

Relationship between pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles:

\\[ PV = nRT \\]

Where:

  • \\(P\\) = pressure
  • \\(V\\) = volume
  • \\(n\\) = number of moles
  • \\(R = 8.314 \, \mathrm{J/mol \cdot K}\\) (universal gas constant)
  • \\(T\\) = temperature in Kelvin
2 moles of gas occupy 10 L at 300 K. Find pressure: \\( P = \frac{nRT}{V} = \frac{2 \cdot 8.314 \cdot 300}{0.01} = 498840 \, \mathrm{Pa} \\)

3. Kinetic Energy of Gas Molecules

The average translational kinetic energy per molecule:

\\[ \langle E_k \rangle = \frac{3}{2} k_B T \\]

Where \\(k_B = 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \mathrm{J/K}\\) is Boltzmann constant.

For n moles:

\\[ E_\text{total} = \frac{3}{2} n R T \\]

4. Root Mean Square Speed

\\[ v_\text{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}} \\]

Where \\(M\\) is molar mass (kg/mol). Example: Oxygen at 300K:

\\( M = 0.032 \, \mathrm{kg/mol}, \quad v_\text{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3 \cdot 8.314 \cdot 300}{0.032}} \approx 517 \, \mathrm{m/s} \\)

5. Maxwell-Boltzmann Distribution

Describes the distribution of molecular speeds in a gas:

\\[ f(v) = 4\pi \left( \frac{M}{2\pi RT} \right)^{3/2} v^2 e^{-\frac{Mv^2}{2RT}} \\]

Shows most probable, mean, and RMS speeds.

6. Real Gases

Deviations from ideal behavior at high pressures and low temperatures. Van der Waals equation:

\\[ \left( P + \frac{an^2}{V^2} \right)(V - nb) = nRT \\]

a = intermolecular attraction constant, b = molecular volume correction.

7. Applications

  • Understanding gas behavior in engines and atmosphere
  • Calculating molecular speeds and effusion rates
  • Thermodynamic properties of gases in chemical reactions
