Oscillations

Simple Harmonic Motion, Damped and Driven Oscillators, Resonance

1. Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Oscillatory motion where the restoring force is proportional to displacement:

\\[ F = -kx, \quad ma = -kx \\quad \Rightarrow \quad a = -\\frac{k}{m} x \\]

General solution:

\\[ x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi), \quad \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \\]
  • Amplitude \\(A\\)
  • Angular frequency \\(\\omega\\)
  • Phase \\(\\phi\\)
  • Period \\(T = 2\\pi / \\omega\\)
Example: A spring with k = 200 N/m is attached to a mass m = 2 kg. Find the angular frequency and period.
\\( \omega = \sqrt{k/m} = \sqrt{200/2} = 10 \, rad/s \\)
\\( T = 2\pi / \omega = 2\pi / 10 \approx 0.628 \, s \\)

2. Energy in SHM

  • Kinetic Energy: \\( KE = \frac12 m v^2 = \frac12 m \omega^2 (A^2 - x^2) \\)
  • Potential Energy: \\( PE = \frac12 k x^2 \\)
  • Total Energy: \\( E = KE + PE = \frac12 k A^2 \\) (constant)

3. Damped Oscillations

Energy is lost due to friction/resistance. Equation:

\\[ m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = 0 \\]
  • Underdamped: oscillatory decay \\( x(t) = A e^{-bt/2m} \cos(\omega' t + \phi) \\)
  • Critically damped: fastest return to equilibrium without oscillation
  • Overdamped: slow return without oscillation

4. Driven Oscillations & Resonance

An external periodic force can drive the system:

\\[ m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = F_0 \cos(\omega t) \\]

Resonance occurs when the driving frequency matches the natural frequency:

\\[ \omega_{res} \approx \omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m} \\]

Applications: musical instruments, bridges, shock absorbers.

5. Applications

  • Clocks: pendulum oscillations
  • Vehicle suspensions: damped oscillations
  • Seismology: measuring earthquakes
  • Engineering: resonance avoidance in buildings and machinery
