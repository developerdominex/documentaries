Relativity

Understanding spacetime, time dilation, length contraction, and the curvature of gravity.

1. Postulates of Special Relativity

Einstein proposed two principles:

  1. The laws of physics are identical in all inertial reference frames.
  2. The speed of light in a vacuum, \( c = 3 \times 10^8\, \text{m/s} \), is constant for all observers.

2. Time Dilation

Moving clocks run slower relative to stationary observers:

\( t' = \frac{t}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \)

3. Length Contraction

The length of a moving object along its direction of motion appears shorter:

\( L' = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} \)

4. Mass–Energy Equivalence

Perhaps the most famous equation in physics, showing the relationship between mass and energy:

\( E = mc^2 \)

5. General Relativity

General Relativity extends these ideas to include gravity as the curvature of spacetime:

\( G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu} \)

Massive objects curve spacetime, and this curvature guides the motion of other objects — explaining phenomena like gravitational lensing and black holes.

6. Applications

  • GPS systems require relativistic time corrections for accuracy.
  • Particle accelerators confirm mass increase with velocity.
  • Gravitational waves observed by LIGO confirm General Relativity.
  • Astrophysics — used to describe black holes and cosmology.
