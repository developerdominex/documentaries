Waves

Wave types, superposition, interference, standing waves, Doppler effect.

1. Types of Waves

  • Mechanical Waves: Require a medium, e.g., sound, water waves.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: No medium needed, e.g., light, radio.
  • Transverse Waves: Particles move perpendicular to wave propagation.
  • Longitudinal Waves: Particles move parallel to wave propagation.

2. Wave Equation

Relationship between speed, frequency, and wavelength:

\\[ v = f \lambda \\]

Where \\(v\\) is wave speed, \\(f\\) is frequency, \\(\\lambda\\) is wavelength.

Example: A wave with frequency 500 Hz and wavelength 0.6 m: \\( v = f \\lambda = 500 \\cdot 0.6 = 300 \\, \\mathrm{m/s} \\)

3. Superposition Principle

When two or more waves meet, the resultant displacement is the sum of individual displacements:

\\[ y_\text{res} = y_1 + y_2 + \\dots \\]

4. Interference

  • Constructive: Waves in phase, amplitudes add.
  • Destructive: Waves out of phase, amplitudes subtract.

Path difference \\(\\Delta L\\) for constructive interference:

\\[ \\Delta L = n\\lambda, \\quad n = 0,1,2,... \\]

Path difference for destructive interference:

\\[ \\Delta L = \\left(n + \\frac{1}{2}\\right)\\lambda \\]

5. Standing Waves

Formed by the superposition of two waves traveling in opposite directions:

\\[ y(x,t) = 2A \\sin(kx) \\cos(\\omega t) \\]

Nodes: Points with zero displacement; Antinodes: Points with maximum displacement.

Frequency of nth harmonic on a string of length L:

\\[ f_n = \\frac{n v}{2 L}, \\quad n = 1,2,3,... \\]

6. Doppler Effect

Change in observed frequency due to relative motion between source and observer:

\\[ f' = \\frac{v + v_o}{v - v_s} f \\]

Where \\(f'\\) = observed frequency, \\(v\\) = wave speed, \\(v_o\\) = observer speed, \\(v_s\\) = source speed.

7. Applications

  • Musical instruments and sound waves
  • Seismology: detecting earthquakes using wave propagation
  • Doppler radar for weather and speed detection
  • Optics and interference patterns
