Astrophysics

Connecting the laws of physics to stars, galaxies, and the universe itself.

1. Stellar Structure

Stars are giant nuclear reactors where hydrogen fuses into helium, releasing energy. Their equilibrium is determined by hydrostatic balance:

\( \frac{dP}{dr} = -\frac{G M(r) \rho(r)}{r^2} \)

2. Black Holes

Black holes form when massive stars collapse beyond their Schwarzschild radius:

\( R_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \)

Nothing, not even light, can escape this boundary.

3. Cosmology & the Expanding Universe

The large-scale structure of the universe is described by the Friedmann equations derived from general relativity:

\( \left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G\rho}{3} - \frac{k c^2}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3} \)

Where \( a(t) \) is the scale factor, \( \Lambda \) is the cosmological constant, and \( k \) defines curvature.

4. Dark Matter & Dark Energy

  • Dark Matter — unseen mass influencing galactic rotation.
  • Dark Energy — drives the accelerated expansion of the universe.

5. Applications & Tools

  • Telescopes — optical, radio, X-ray, and gravitational wave observatories.
  • Space missions — Hubble, JWST, Planck, and Gaia.
  • Simulations — model galaxy formation and cosmic evolution.
