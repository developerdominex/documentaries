Particle Physics

Understanding the smallest constituents of matter and the fundamental forces between them.

1. The Standard Model

The Standard Model is the theoretical framework describing all known fundamental particles and their interactions (except gravity). It includes:

  • Quarks — up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom.
  • Leptons — electron, muon, tau, and their neutrinos.
  • Gauge Bosons — photon, W±, Z⁰, gluons, and the Higgs boson.

2. Fundamental Forces

There are four fundamental forces in nature:

  • Gravitational — acts on mass; weakest but long-ranged.
  • Electromagnetic — acts on charge; described by QED.
  • Weak Nuclear — responsible for beta decay.
  • Strong Nuclear — binds quarks inside protons and neutrons.

3. Key Relations

\( E = mc^2 \)     (Mass–Energy Equivalence)
\( F = g_s^2 / (4\pi r^2) \)     (Strong Force Approximation)
\( \sigma \propto \frac{1}{E^2} \)     (Scattering Cross-section)

4. The Higgs Mechanism

The Higgs field gives particles mass via spontaneous symmetry breaking. Its quantum excitation is the Higgs boson, confirmed at CERN in 2012.

5. Applications and Frontiers

  • Particle accelerators — probe subatomic structure (LHC, Fermilab).
  • Medical physics — PET scans use particle interactions.
  • Cosmology — matter-antimatter asymmetry, dark matter candidates.
