Gravitation

Universal Law, Planetary Motion, and Escape Velocity

1. Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

Every point mass attracts every other point mass with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of their separation:

\\[ F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \\]

Where:

  • \\(F\\) = gravitational force
  • \\(G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, N \cdot m^2 / kg^2\\)
  • \\(m_1, m_2\\) = masses of the objects
  • \\(r\\) = distance between their centers

2. Acceleration Due to Gravity

For an object near the Earth's surface:

\\[ g = \frac{GM}{R^2} \\]

Where:

  • \\(M\\) = mass of the Earth
  • \\(R\\) = radius of the Earth
  • \\(g \approx 9.8 \, m/s^2\\)

3. Planetary Motion

Newton’s laws explain Kepler’s laws:

  • Orbital velocity: \\( v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \\)
  • Period of orbit: \\( T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}} \\)
  • Elliptical motion: planets move in ellipses with the sun at one focus

4. Escape Velocity

The minimum velocity needed to escape the gravitational field of a planet:

\\[ v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} \\]

Example:

Earth: \\( M = 5.972 \times 10^{24} kg, R = 6.371 \times 10^6 m \\) \\( v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 5.972 \times 10^{24}}{6.371 \times 10^6}} \approx 11.2 \, km/s \\)

5. Applications

  • Satellite orbits and launching spacecraft
  • Tidal phenomena on Earth and other celestial bodies
  • Predicting planetary positions
  • Space missions: escape velocity calculations
