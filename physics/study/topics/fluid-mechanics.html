Fluid Mechanics

The physics of liquids and gases in motion and equilibrium.

1. Continuity Equation

Expresses conservation of mass for incompressible flow:

\( A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2 \)

where \( A \) is the cross-sectional area and \( v \) is the flow velocity.

2. Bernoulli’s Principle

Bernoulli’s theorem relates pressure, velocity, and height in a streamline:

\( P + \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{constant} \)

This principle explains lift in airplane wings and pressure variations in flowing fluids.

3. Viscosity & Flow Types

Viscosity measures internal resistance to flow. The flow can be:

  • Laminar flow — smooth, orderly motion.
  • Turbulent flow — chaotic, mixing motion.

Newton’s law of viscosity:

\( \tau = \eta \frac{du}{dy} \)

4. Navier–Stokes Equation

Governs the motion of viscous fluids:

\( \rho \left( \frac{\partial \vec{v}}{\partial t} + (\vec{v} \cdot \nabla)\vec{v} \right) = -\nabla P + \eta \nabla^2 \vec{v} + \rho \vec{g} \)

Solving these equations is one of the great challenges in mathematical physics.

5. Applications

  • Designing airplanes, ships, and hydraulic systems.
  • Predicting weather and ocean currents.
  • Understanding blood flow and respiratory systems.
