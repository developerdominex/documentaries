Thermodynamics

Heat, Work, Energy, and the Laws of Thermodynamics

1. Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then they are in thermal equilibrium with each other. Basis for temperature measurement.

2. First Law of Thermodynamics

Conservation of energy in thermodynamic processes:

\\[ \Delta U = Q - W \\]

Where:

  • \\(\\Delta U\\) = change in internal energy
  • \\(Q\\) = heat added to the system
  • \\(W\\) = work done by the system
A gas absorbs 500 J of heat and does 200 J of work. Find the change in internal energy: \\( \Delta U = 500 - 200 = 300 \, J \\)

3. Second Law of Thermodynamics

Entropy of an isolated system never decreases. Heat cannot spontaneously flow from cold to hot.

\\[ \Delta S \ge 0 \\]

Applications:

  • Efficiency limits of engines
  • Direction of spontaneous processes

4. Third Law of Thermodynamics

As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero.

\\[ \lim_{T \to 0} S = 0 \\]

5. Thermodynamic Processes

  • Isothermal: \\( \Delta U = 0, Q = W \\)
  • Adiabatic: \\( Q = 0, \Delta U = -W \\)
  • Isobaric: Constant pressure, \\( W = P \Delta V \\)
  • Isochoric: Constant volume, \\( W = 0, \Delta U = Q \\)

6. Heat Engines and Efficiency

Efficiency of a heat engine:

\\[ \eta = \frac{W}{Q_H} = 1 - \frac{Q_C}{Q_H} \\]

Where \\(Q_H\\) = heat absorbed, \\(Q_C\\) = heat rejected.

A Carnot engine absorbs 1000 J and rejects 400 J. Efficiency: \\( \eta = 1 - 400/1000 = 0.6 = 60\% \\)

7. Applications

  • Refrigerators and air conditioners
  • Steam engines and power plants
  • Thermal efficiency optimization
  • Understanding natural phenomena (entropy, heat transfer)
