Mechanics

Motion, Forces, Newton's Laws, Work, Energy, Momentum

1. Motion

Mechanics begins with the study of motion of objects.

  • Displacement: Change in position, \\( s = s_2 - s_1 \\)
  • Velocity: Rate of change of displacement, \\( v = \\frac{ds}{dt} \\)
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity, \\( a = \\frac{dv}{dt} \\)
Equations of motion for constant acceleration:
\\[ v = u + at, \quad s = ut + \\frac{1}{2} a t^2, \quad v^2 = u^2 + 2as \\]
Example: A car starts from rest and accelerates at \\( 2 \, m/s^2 \\). Find the velocity after 5 s.
Solution: \\( v = u + at = 0 + 2 \cdot 5 = 10 \, m/s \\)

2. Forces and Newton's Laws

Forces cause motion, described by Newton's Laws:

  • First Law: Inertia: an object remains at rest or moves uniformly unless acted upon by a force.
  • Second Law: \\( F = ma \\)
  • Third Law: Action and reaction are equal and opposite.
Example: A 5 kg object is pushed with a force of 20 N. Find acceleration.
Solution: \\( a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 \, m/s^2 \\)

3. Work, Energy, and Power

  • Work: \\( W = F \cdot d \cdot \cos\theta \\)
  • Kinetic Energy: \\( KE = \\frac12 mv^2 \\)
  • Potential Energy: \\( PE = mgh \\)
  • Power: \\( P = \\frac{dW}{dt} \\)
Example: A 10 kg object is lifted 5 m vertically. Find work done and potential energy.
\\( W = PE = mgh = 10 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 5 = 490 \, J \\)

4. Momentum and Impulse

  • Momentum: \\( p = mv \\)
  • Impulse: \\( J = F \Delta t = \Delta p \\)
  • Conservation of Momentum: Total momentum before collision = total momentum after.
Example: Two balls of masses 2 kg and 3 kg move towards each other at 5 m/s and 2 m/s. Find final velocities after elastic collision.
(Solution requires solving using conservation laws.)

5. Applications

  • Engineering: Designing machines, vehicles, bridges.
  • Aerospace: Rocket motion, satellite trajectories.
  • Sports: Analysis of motion and forces in athletics.
  • Daily life: Lifting, driving, riding, and impact calculations.
