Differential Geometry

Curves, surfaces, curvature, and manifolds

Introduction

Differential geometry studies curves, surfaces, and higher-dimensional manifolds using techniques of calculus and linear algebra. It connects geometry with analysis and has applications in physics, engineering, and computer graphics.

Curves

A curve in space can be represented parametrically as \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (x(t), y(t), z(t)) \). Key concepts:

  • Tangent vector: \( \mathbf{T} = \frac{d\mathbf{r}}{dt} / \|\frac{d\mathbf{r}}{dt}\| \)
  • Curvature: \( \kappa = \|\mathbf{T}'(s)\| \), measures bending
  • Torsion: \( \tau \), measures twisting of the curve
Example: Circle of radius R: \( \mathbf{r}(t) = (R\cos t, R\sin t) \), curvature \( \kappa = 1/R \)

Surfaces

A surface can be parametrized as \( \mathbf{X}(u,v) = (x(u,v), y(u,v), z(u,v)) \). Key quantities:

  • First fundamental form: \( E = \mathbf{X}_u \cdot \mathbf{X}_u, F = \mathbf{X}_u \cdot \mathbf{X}_v, G = \mathbf{X}_v \cdot \mathbf{X}_v \)
  • Second fundamental form: measures curvature of the surface
  • Gaussian curvature \( K = \frac{LN - M^2}{EG - F^2} \)

Example surface with tangent directions

Manifolds

A manifold is a topological space that locally resembles Euclidean space. Examples include curves, surfaces, and higher-dimensional spaces.

Example: The surface of a sphere \( S^2 \) is a 2-dimensional manifold.

Applications

  • General Relativity: spacetime as a 4D manifold with curvature
  • Computer graphics: modeling curves and surfaces
  • Robotics: path planning on manifolds
  • Engineering: stress analysis on curved surfaces

History

Differential geometry developed in the 18th and 19th centuries with contributions from Gauss, Riemann, Frenet, and Cartan. It bridges classical geometry and modern physics.
