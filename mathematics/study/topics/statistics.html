Statistics

Understanding data through collection, organization, analysis, and interpretation.

Introduction to Statistics

Statistics is the branch of mathematics that deals with data collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation. It helps in making informed decisions using numerical evidence.

It is widely used in fields like economics, science, education, and business for making predictions and understanding trends.

Measures of Central Tendency

These are values that represent the center or average of a data set.

  • Mean: The sum of all observations divided by the number of observations.
  • Median: The middle value when all data are arranged in order.
  • Mode: The most frequently occurring value in a dataset.
Example: For data 2, 4, 6, 8, 10:
Mean = (2+4+6+8+10)/5 = 6
Median = 6 (middle value)
Mode = None (no repetition)

Measures of Dispersion

These indicate how spread out the data values are.

  • Range: Difference between the highest and lowest values.
  • Variance: The average of the squared deviations from the mean.
  • Standard Deviation: The square root of variance.

Probability Basics

Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

Example: The probability of getting a head when tossing a fair coin is 1/2.

Applications of Statistics

  • Predicting economic trends
  • Analyzing scientific data
  • Quality control in industries
  • Public health and education planning

Conclusion

Statistics allows us to summarize large amounts of data, identify patterns, and make reasoned decisions. It is a foundational tool in modern scientific, social, and business research.
