1. Overview

Trigonometry studies relationships between angles and side lengths in triangles and the properties of trigonometric functions such as sine, cosine and tangent. It is essential in geometry, physics, engineering, signal processing, and more.

Basic Definitions

Consider a right triangle with acute angle θ. The three primary trigonometric ratios are:

  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite / adjacent = sin θ / cos θ
Example: In a right triangle with sides 3 (opposite), 4 (adjacent), 5 (hypotenuse): sin θ = 3/5 = 0.6; cos θ = 4/5 = 0.8; tan θ = 3/4 = 0.75.

Reciprocal Functions

  • csc θ = 1 / sin θ
  • sec θ = 1 / cos θ
  • cot θ = 1 / tan θ

2. Unit Circle (Core Concept)

The unit circle is the circle of radius 1 centered at the origin in the Cartesian plane. It links angles (measured from the positive x-axis) to coordinates: a point at angle θ on the unit circle has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ).

On the unit circle: angle → coordinates. For θ = 30°, cos θ = √3/2 ≈ 0.866, sin θ = 1/2 = 0.5.

Use the unit circle to define trig functions for all real angles and to understand sign changes by quadrant.

3. Trigonometric Identities (Important)

Basic identities you should know:

Pythagorean:
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Angle addition:
sin(a±b)=sin a cos b ± cos a sin b
Angle addition:
cos(a±b)=cos a cos b ∓ sin a sin b
Double-angle:
sin 2a = 2 sin a cos a
Double-angle:
cos 2a = cos²a − sin²a = 2cos²a − 1 = 1 − 2sin²a
Tangent:
tan(a±b) = (tan a ± tan b)/(1 ∓ tan a tan b)

Derived identities

From sin²θ + cos²θ = 1, divide by cos²θ to get 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ. Divide by sin²θ to get cot²θ + 1 = csc²θ.

5. Solving Trigonometric Equations

Equations like sin x = 0.5 have infinitely many solutions. Use general solutions:

Example: Solve sin x = 1/2.

Primary solutions: x = π/6 + 2kπ and x = 5π/6 + 2kπ for any integer k.

6. Inverse Trigonometric Functions

Arcsin, arccos, arctan give principal values (inverse functions on restricted domains):

  • arcsin : [−1,1] → [−π/2, π/2]
  • arccos : [−1,1] → [0, π]
  • arctan : ℝ → (−π/2, π/2)

7. Identities & Useful Formulas (Quick Reference)

sin(−θ) = −sin θ
cos(−θ) = cos θ
tan(−θ) = −tan θ
sin(π/2 − θ) = cos θ
cos(π/2 − θ) = sin θ
sin(a)cos(b) = ½[sin(a+b)+sin(a−b)]

9. Examples & Exercises

1) Compute sin 45°, cos 45°, tan 45°.

Since a 45°-45°-90° triangle has legs equal, sin45° = cos45° = √2/2 ≈ 0.707; tan45° = 1.

2) Prove: sin²θ = (1 − cos 2θ)/2.

Use cos 2θ = 1 − 2sin²θ ⇒ rearrange to get sin²θ = (1 − cos 2θ)/2.

3) Given triangle with sides 7 (adjacent), 24 (opposite), find angle θ and hypotenuse.

Hypotenuse = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25. sin θ = 24/25; θ ≈ arcsin(24/25) ≈ 73.74°.

10. Historical Notes

Trigonometry developed from astronomy and geometry in ancient civilizations. Egyptians and Babylonians used early trig ideas. Hellenistic mathematicians established chord tables; Indian and Islamic mathematicians later developed sine and cosine. The modern symbolic trigonometry system was assembled between the medieval Islamic period and Renaissance Europe.
