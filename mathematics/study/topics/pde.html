Partial Differential Equations

Wave, heat, and Navier–Stokes equations

Introduction

Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) involve functions of multiple variables and their partial derivatives. They describe many physical phenomena such as heat conduction, waves, and fluid flow.

Common Types of PDEs

  • Heat Equation: Describes diffusion of heat or other substances.
    u_t = α ∇²u
  • Wave Equation: Describes vibrations and wave propagation.
    u_tt = c² ∇²u
  • Laplace Equation: Steady-state solutions, potential theory.
    ∇²u = 0
  • Poisson Equation: Generalization of Laplace equation.
    ∇²u = f(x,y,...)
  • Navier–Stokes Equations: Fluid motion and dynamics.
    ρ(∂v/∂t + v·∇v) = -∇p + μ∇²v + f

Methods of Solution

  • Separation of variables
  • Fourier series and transforms
  • Laplace transforms
  • Green’s functions
  • Numerical methods: Finite difference, Finite element, Spectral methods

Examples

Solve the 1D heat equation u_t = α u_xx on a rod of length L with boundary conditions u(0,t)=0, u(L,t)=0.
Solution using separation of variables: u(x,t) = Σ B_n sin(nπx/L) e^(-α(nπ/L)² t)
Wave equation example: u_tt = c² u_xx with fixed ends u(0,t)=u(L,t)=0.
Solution: u(x,t) = Σ (A_n cos(nπct/L) + B_n sin(nπct/L)) sin(nπx/L)

Applications

  • Physics: heat conduction, wave propagation, quantum mechanics
  • Engineering: structural analysis, fluid dynamics, aerodynamics
  • Finance: Black–Scholes equation for option pricing
  • Biology: population models and diffusion processes

History

PDEs have been studied since the 18th century, with contributions by Euler, Lagrange, Fourier, and Navier. They remain central to applied mathematics and scientific modeling.
