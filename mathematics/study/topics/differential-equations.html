Differential Equations

Ordinary and partial differential equations, methods and applications.

Introduction

A differential equation (DE) is an equation involving a function and its derivatives. DEs are used to model change and describe natural phenomena such as population growth, physics motion, and heat transfer.

Example: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 3y\) describes exponential growth or decay.

Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)

ODEs involve derivatives with respect to a single independent variable.

  • First-order: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x,y)\)
  • Second-order: \(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + p(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + q(x)y = r(x)\)
  • Higher-order: Involves derivatives of order 3 or more
Solve \(\frac{dy}{dx} = ky\) → \(y = Ce^{kx}\)

Methods of Solving ODEs

  • Separable equations: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = g(x)h(y)\) → \(\int \frac{1}{h(y)} dy = \int g(x) dx\)
  • Linear equations: \(\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)\) → using integrating factor \( \mu(x) = e^{\int P(x) dx} \)
  • Exact equations: \(M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0\) → check \(\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}\)

Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)

PDEs involve derivatives with respect to multiple independent variables.

  • Example: Heat equation \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}\)
  • Wave equation \(\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}\)
  • Laplace equation \(\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = 0\)

Applications

  • Physics: Motion, oscillations, circuits
  • Biology: Population dynamics
  • Engineering: Heat transfer, fluid flow
  • Economics: Growth and decay models

History

Differential equations were developed in the 17th century by Newton and Leibniz along with calculus. They are fundamental in describing dynamic systems in science and engineering.
