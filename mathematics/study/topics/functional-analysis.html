Functional Analysis

Banach and Hilbert spaces, operators, and spectral theory

Introduction

Functional Analysis is the study of vector spaces with limit-related structures (like norms and inner products) and the linear operators acting on them. It provides the foundation for modern analysis, quantum mechanics, and PDE theory.

Banach Spaces

A Banach space is a complete normed vector space. Completeness means every Cauchy sequence converges within the space.

Example: The space of continuous functions C([0,1]) with the sup norm ||f||∞ = max |f(x)| is a Banach space.

Hilbert Spaces

A Hilbert space is a complete inner product space. The inner product allows definitions of length and angles.

Example: L²([0,1]) = {f : ∫₀¹ |f(x)|² dx < ∞} with ⟨f,g⟩ = ∫₀¹ f(x)g(x) dx is a Hilbert space.

Linear Operators

Linear operators map elements of a vector space to another (or the same) vector space while preserving addition and scalar multiplication.

Example: T: L²([0,1]) → L²([0,1]), Tf(x) = ∫₀ˣ f(t) dt

Spectral Theory

Spectral theory studies eigenvalues and eigenvectors of linear operators, particularly on infinite-dimensional spaces.

Example: For compact operator T on Hilbert space H, spectrum σ(T) consists of eigenvalues λ with T(v)=λv.

Applications

  • Quantum mechanics: Hilbert spaces for wavefunctions
  • Partial differential equations: operator theory for solution analysis
  • Signal processing: Fourier and functional transforms
  • Numerical analysis: stability and convergence of algorithms

History

Functional analysis emerged in the early 20th century from the work of Fréchet, Banach, and Hilbert. It formalized the study of infinite-dimensional spaces and became central in modern analysis.
