Real Analysis

Limits, continuity, series, measure, and integration theory

Introduction

Real analysis deals with real numbers, sequences, series, functions, limits, continuity, and the rigorous foundations of calculus. It provides tools to study convergence and measure theory, ensuring mathematical precision.

Limits and Continuity

  • Limit of a function: \( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \)
  • Continuity: \( f \) is continuous at \( x=a \) if \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\)
  • Discontinuities: removable, jump, essential
Example: \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \)

Sequences and Series

  • Convergent and divergent sequences: \( \lim_{n\to\infty} a_n \)
  • Series: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \), convergence tests (comparison, ratio, root)
  • Power series and Taylor series
Example: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \)

Measure Theory

Measure theory generalizes the concept of length, area, and volume for complex sets. It defines measurable sets and functions.

  • Lebesgue measure
  • Measurable functions and integration

Integration Theory

  • Riemann integral: \( \int_a^b f(x) dx \)
  • Lebesgue integral: allows integration of more general functions
  • Fundamental theorem of calculus
Riemann example: \( \int_0^1 x^2 dx = \frac{1}{3} \)

Applications

  • Mathematical analysis of functions
  • Convergence of series in physics and engineering
  • Probability theory and measure-theoretic foundations
  • Advanced calculus and PDE analysis

History

Real analysis was formalized in the 19th century by mathematicians such as Augustin-Louis Cauchy, Karl Weierstrass, and Henri Lebesgue, giving rigorous foundations to calculus and integration theory.
