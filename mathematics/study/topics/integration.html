Integration

Integrals, area under curves, substitution, parts, and applications.

Introduction to Integration

Integration is the reverse process of differentiation. It is used to find the area under a curve, accumulated quantities, or to reconstruct a function from its derivative.

Example: If \( f(x) = 2x \), then the integral is \[ \int 2x \, dx = x^2 + C \] where \(C\) is the constant of integration.

Indefinite Integrals

  • Power rule: \(\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C, n \neq -1\)
  • Exponential: \(\int e^x \, dx = e^x + C\)
  • Trigonometric: \(\int \sin x \, dx = -\cos x + C\), \(\int \cos x \, dx = \sin x + C\)
  • Sum/difference: \(\int (f \pm g) dx = \int f dx \pm \int g dx\)
\(\int (3x^2 + 4) dx = x^3 + 4x + C\)

Definite Integrals

Definite integrals compute the area under a curve from \(a\) to \(b\):

\[ \int_a^b f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a) \]
\(\int_0^2 x^2 dx = \left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2 = \frac{8}{3}\)

Techniques of Integration

  • Substitution: Let \( u = g(x) \), then \(\int f(g(x)) g'(x) dx = \int f(u) du\)
  • Integration by Parts: \(\int u dv = uv - \int v du\)
  • Partial Fractions: Decompose rational functions into simpler fractions for integration
  • Trigonometric Integrals: Use trig identities to simplify integrals
\(\int x e^x dx = x e^x - \int e^x dx = x e^x - e^x + C\)

Applications of Integration

  • Area under curves
  • Volume of solids of revolution
  • Work done by a variable force
  • Center of mass and moments
  • Probability and statistics (continuous distributions)

Definite Integral Example with Area

Find the area under \( y = x^2 \) from \( x = 0 \) to \( x = 3 \): \[ \int_0^3 x^2 dx = \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_0^3 = \frac{27}{3} - 0 = 9 \]
