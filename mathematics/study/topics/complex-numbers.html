Complex Numbers

Imaginary numbers, polar form, Euler’s formula, and roots of unity.

Introduction

Complex numbers are numbers that include the imaginary unit i, where i² = -1. They are written in the form z = a + bi, where a is the real part and b is the imaginary part.

Example:
z = 3 + 4i → Real part = 3, Imaginary part = 4

Operations with Complex Numbers

  • Addition: (a+bi) + (c+di) = (a+c) + (b+d)i
  • Subtraction: (a+bi) - (c+di) = (a-c) + (b-d)i
  • Multiplication: (a+bi)(c+di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i
  • Division: (a+bi)/(c+di) = [(ac + bd) + (bc - ad)i]/(c² + d²)
(2 + 3i) + (4 - i) = 6 + 2i
(1 + 2i)(3 + 4i) = 3 + 4i + 6i + 8i² = -5 + 10i

Polar Form

A complex number can also be expressed in polar form: z = r(cosθ + i sinθ), where r = |z| = √(a² + b²) is the magnitude and θ = arctan(b/a) is the argument (angle).

Magnitude: r = √(a² + b²)
Angle: θ = arctan(b/a)
z = 1 + i → r = √2, θ = 45°

Euler’s Formula

Euler’s formula links complex numbers and exponential functions:

e^(iθ) = cosθ + i sinθ

z = r e^(iθ) → Equivalent to z = r(cosθ + i sinθ)

Roots of Unity

Roots of unity are solutions to the equation zⁿ = 1. They are equally spaced points on the unit circle in the complex plane.

1 → 0°
i → 90°
-1 → 180°
-i → 270°
4th roots of unity: 1, i, -1, -i

Applications

  • Electrical engineering (AC circuits)
  • Quantum mechanics
  • Signal processing
  • Fractals and geometry
