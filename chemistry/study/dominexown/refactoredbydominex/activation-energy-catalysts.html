Activation Energy and Catalysts

Activation Energy (Ea)

The minimum energy required for a reaction to occur.

Determines the rate of reaction according to Arrhenius equation:

k = A e^(-Ea/RT)

Catalysts

Catalysts increase the rate by providing an alternative pathway with lower Ea.

Not consumed in the reaction and do not change the equilibrium position.

Example

  • Decomposition of H₂O₂ with MnO₂ as catalyst
  • Enzymes in biological reactions
