Atomic Radius

Atomic radius is the distance from the nucleus to the outermost electron.

Trends:

  • Decreases across a period (electrons pulled closer by increasing nuclear charge).
  • Increases down a group (addition of electron shells).

Ionization Energy

The energy required to remove an electron from a gaseous atom.

Trends:

  • Increases across a period (electrons more tightly bound).
  • Decreases down a group (outer electrons farther from nucleus).

Electronegativity

Electronegativity is the tendency of an atom to attract electrons in a bond.

Trends:

  • Increases across a period.
  • Decreases down a group.
  • Fluorine is the most electronegative element.

Electron Affinity

Electron affinity is the energy change when an atom gains an electron.

  • More negative values indicate stronger attraction for electrons.
  • Tends to become more negative across a period.
  • Tends to become less negative down a group.

Metallic Character

Metallic character describes how readily an element loses electrons to form cations.

  • Decreases across a period.
  • Increases down a group.
  • Correlates with conductivity, malleability, and luster.

Reactivity Trends

Reactivity varies depending on metallic or non-metallic nature:

  • Metals: Reactivity increases down a group (e.g., alkali metals).
  • Nonmetals: Reactivity decreases down a group (e.g., halogens).
