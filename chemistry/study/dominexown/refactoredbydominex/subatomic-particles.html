⚛️ Subatomic Particles

Protons

Protons are positively charged particles found in the nucleus of an atom. They have a relative charge of +1 and a mass of approximately 1 atomic mass unit (amu). The number of protons defines the atomic number of an element and its identity.

Key Properties:

  • Charge: +1
  • Mass: ~1 amu
  • Location: Nucleus
  • Determines element identity

Neutrons

Neutrons are neutral particles, also found in the nucleus. They have no charge and a mass slightly greater than that of protons. Neutrons play a key role in the stability of the nucleus and isotopic variations of elements.

Key Properties:

  • Charge: 0
  • Mass: ~1 amu
  • Location: Nucleus
  • Determines isotopes of elements

Electrons

Electrons are negatively charged particles that orbit the nucleus in electron shells. They are much lighter than protons or neutrons and determine the chemical behavior of atoms by forming bonds.

Key Properties:

  • Charge: −1
  • Mass: ~1/1836 amu
  • Location: Electron cloud around nucleus
  • Responsible for chemical bonding
