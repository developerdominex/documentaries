Real Gases

Introduction

Real gases deviate from ideal behavior at high pressure and low temperature.

Van der Waals Equation

Accounts for particle volume and intermolecular attraction:

(P + a(n²/V²)) (V - nb) = nRT

where a corrects for intermolecular forces and b for particle volume.

Behavior of Real Gases

At low pressure and high temperature, gases behave ideally. Deviations are significant near condensation points.

Applications

Useful in understanding liquefaction, compressibility, and industrial gas processes.
