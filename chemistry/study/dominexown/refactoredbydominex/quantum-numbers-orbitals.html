⚛️ Quantum Numbers and Electron Orbitals

Overview

Quantum numbers describe the distribution and behavior of electrons in atoms. They are derived from the quantum mechanical model and determine the energy, shape, orientation, and spin of orbitals.

Principal Quantum Number (n)

The principal quantum number n indicates the energy level of an electron in an atom. It can take positive integer values (1, 2, 3…).

  • Determines the size and energy of the orbital
  • Higher n → electrons are further from nucleus → higher energy

Azimuthal Quantum Number (l)

The azimuthal quantum number l determines the shape of the orbital. Its values range from 0 to n-1 for each energy level.

  • l = 0 → s orbital (spherical)
  • l = 1 → p orbital (dumbbell-shaped)
  • l = 2 → d orbital (cloverleaf)
  • l = 3 → f orbital (complex)

Magnetic Quantum Number (ml)

The magnetic quantum number ml defines the orientation of the orbital in space. It can take values from -l to +l, including zero.

Spin Quantum Number (ms)

The spin quantum number ms describes the intrinsic spin of an electron. It has only two possible values: +½ or -½.

Two electrons in the same orbital must have opposite spins (Pauli Exclusion Principle).

Electron Orbitals

Orbitals are regions in an atom where the probability of finding an electron is highest. Their shapes are determined by quantum numbers:

  • s-orbital: spherical, 1 orientation
  • p-orbital: dumbbell-shaped, 3 orientations
  • d-orbital: clover-shaped, 5 orientations
  • f-orbital: complex shape, 7 orientations

Electron Configuration & Aufbau Principle

Electrons fill orbitals starting from the lowest energy level according to the Aufbau principle, Pauli Exclusion Principle, and Hund’s rule.

  • Example: Oxygen → 1s² 2s² 2p⁴
  • Orbitals in order of filling: 1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 4s, 3d, 4p…
