Solids and Crystal Lattices

Introduction

Solids have definite shape and volume. Atoms or molecules are arranged in fixed patterns.

Types of Solids

  • Crystalline solids: regular repeating structure
  • Amorphous solids: irregular structure

Crystal Lattice Structures

  • Simple cubic (SC)
  • Body-centered cubic (BCC)
  • Face-centered cubic (FCC)
  • Hexagonal close-packed (HCP)

Unit Cell and Coordination Number

Unit cell: smallest repeating structure. Coordination number: number of nearest neighbors surrounding a particle.

Properties

Melting point, hardness, conductivity, and density depend on crystal structure.
