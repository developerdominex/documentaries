Gas Laws

Introduction

Gas laws describe how gases behave with changes in pressure, volume, and temperature.

Boyle’s Law

At constant temperature, pressure is inversely proportional to volume:

P ∝ 1/V or P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

Charles’s Law

At constant pressure, volume is directly proportional to temperature:

V ∝ T or V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

Gay-Lussac’s Law

At constant volume, pressure is directly proportional to temperature:

P ∝ T or P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂

Avogadro’s Law

At constant temperature and pressure, volume is proportional to number of moles:

V ∝ n

Ideal Gas Law

Combining all gas laws:

PV = nRT
