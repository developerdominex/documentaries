⚛ Molecular Orbital Theory

Definition

Molecular Orbital Theory describes the formation of molecular orbitals by combining atomic orbitals, providing a more accurate depiction of electron distribution in molecules.

Bonding and Antibonding

Electrons occupy bonding orbitals to stabilize the molecule and antibonding orbitals to destabilize. Bond order = (bonding electrons - antibonding electrons)/2.

Applications

MOT explains magnetic properties, bond length, bond energy, and electronic structure of diatomic and polyatomic molecules — useful in spectroscopy and molecular modeling.
