Factors Affecting Solubility

Introduction

Solubility is the maximum amount of solute that can dissolve in a solvent at given conditions. Several factors influence solubility.

Factors Affecting Solubility

  • Nature of solute and solvent: "Like dissolves like" principle – polar solutes dissolve in polar solvents, non-polar in non-polar.
  • Temperature: Solubility of solids generally increases with temperature; gases usually decrease solubility as temperature rises.
  • Pressure: Mainly affects gases; solubility increases with pressure (Henry’s Law).
  • Presence of other substances: Common ion effect may decrease solubility.
