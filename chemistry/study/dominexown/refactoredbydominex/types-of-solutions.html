Types of Solutions

Introduction

A solution is a homogeneous mixture of two or more substances. Solutions can exist in various states: solid, liquid, or gas.

Types of Solutions Based on Solvent

  • Liquid solutions: Solid, liquid, or gas dissolved in a liquid (e.g., salt in water).
  • Solid solutions: Metals or alloys (e.g., brass, an alloy of copper and zinc).
  • Gaseous solutions: Gas mixtures (e.g., air, a mixture of oxygen and nitrogen).

Types Based on Concentration

  • Dilute solutions: Small amount of solute in solvent.
  • Concentrated solutions: Large amount of solute in solvent.
  • Saturated solutions: Maximum solute dissolved at given conditions.
  • Unsaturated solutions: Less than maximum solute dissolved.
  • Supersaturated solutions: More solute dissolved than normally possible under equilibrium.
