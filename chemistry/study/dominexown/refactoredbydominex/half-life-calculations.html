Half-Life Calculations

Definition

Half-life (t½) is the time required for half the nuclei in a radioactive sample to decay.

Formula

For first-order decay: N = N₀ e^(-λt)

Where N = remaining nuclei, N₀ = initial nuclei, λ = decay constant, t = time.

Half-life: t½ = 0.693 / λ

Applications

  • Determining age of fossils (radiocarbon dating)
  • Medical diagnostics and treatment
  • Estimating decay of nuclear waste
