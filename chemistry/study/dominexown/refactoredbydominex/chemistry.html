States of Matter and Kinetic Theory

Matter exists primarily in four states: solid, liquid, gas, and plasma. Each state corresponds to energy levels and particle arrangements.

  • Solids: Fixed shape and volume; strong intermolecular forces.
  • Liquids: Fixed volume but variable shape; particles slide past each other.
  • Gases: No fixed shape or volume; expand to fill container.
  • Plasmas: Ionized gases found in stars and lightning.

Kinetic molecular theory relates temperature to particle motion. Phase changes ‚Äî melting, evaporation, condensation, and sublimation ‚Äî occur when energy input alters intermolecular forces.
