Le Chatelier's Principle

Introduction

Le Chatelierâ€™s Principle states that if a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it will adjust to counteract the disturbance and restore equilibrium.

Factors Affecting Equilibrium

  • Concentration: Adding reactants shifts equilibrium to products; removing shifts it to reactants.
  • Pressure: Increasing pressure shifts equilibrium toward side with fewer gas molecules.
  • Temperature: Exothermic reaction favors reactants when heated; endothermic favors products.

Applications

  • Industrial chemical processes like Haber process
  • Predicting shifts in chemical equilibria
