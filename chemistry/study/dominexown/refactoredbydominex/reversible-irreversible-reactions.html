Reversible and Irreversible Reactions

Introduction

Chemical reactions can be classified as reversible or irreversible based on whether products can revert back to reactants.

Irreversible Reactions

  • Proceed in one direction only.
  • Reactants completely convert to products.
  • Example: Combustion of methane: CH₄ + 2 O₂ → CO₂ + 2 H₂O

Reversible Reactions

  • Can proceed in both forward and backward directions.
  • Eventually reach dynamic equilibrium where the rate of forward reaction equals the rate of reverse reaction.
  • Example: N₂ + 3 H₂ ⇌ 2 NH₃
