Raoult's Law

Introduction

Raoult's Law relates the vapor pressure of a solution to the mole fraction of its components.

Law Statement

The partial vapor pressure of a component in a solution is proportional to its mole fraction:

P_A = X_A × P⁰_A

Where P_A is the vapor pressure of component A, X_A is its mole fraction, and P⁰_A is the vapor pressure of pure A.

Applications

  • Determining molecular weights of solutes.
  • Understanding colligative properties.
  • Predicting behavior of ideal solutions.
