Rate Laws and Order of Reaction

Rate Law

Rate law expresses the rate as a function of concentration:

Rate = k [A]^m [B]^n

k = rate constant, m & n = orders with respect to A and B

Order of Reaction

Total order = m + n

  • Zero order: Rate independent of concentration
  • First order: Rate ∝ [reactant]
  • Second order: Rate ∝ [reactant]² or [A][B]

Example

Reaction: 2 NO + O₂ → 2 NO₂
