Kinetic Theory of Gases

Introduction

The kinetic theory explains the macroscopic properties of gases, like pressure and temperature, in terms of the microscopic motion of gas particles.

Assumptions of Kinetic Theory

  • Gas consists of a large number of tiny particles in constant random motion.
  • Collisions between particles and with container walls are perfectly elastic.
  • Volume of gas particles is negligible compared to the container volume.
  • No intermolecular forces except during collisions.
