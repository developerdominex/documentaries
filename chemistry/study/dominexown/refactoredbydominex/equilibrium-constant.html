Chemical Equilibrium Constant

Introduction

The equilibrium constant quantifies the ratio of products to reactants at equilibrium for a reversible reaction.

Expression

For the reaction:

aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

The equilibrium constant (K) is:

K_c = [C]^c [D]^d / [A]^a [B]^b

Where [ ] represents equilibrium molar concentrations.

Significance

  • K > 1: products favored
  • K < 1: reactants favored
  • K ≈ 1: neither side strongly favored
