Early Attempts at Classification

Before Mendeleev, chemists attempted to organize elements based on atomic mass, chemical properties, and reactivity.

  • Dmitri Mendeleev (1869) arranged elements by increasing atomic mass and left gaps for undiscovered elements.
  • Lothar Meyer independently noted periodicity of properties but published after Mendeleev.
  • Early systems highlighted the need for a systematic framework in chemistry.

Modern Periodic Table

The modern periodic table is organized by atomic number rather than atomic mass, reflecting the number of protons in each element.

  • Horizontal rows: periods, vertical columns: groups
  • Elements exhibit periodic recurrence of properties across periods
  • Lanthanides and actinides are displayed separately to maintain table structure

Periodic Law

The periodic law states that the properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic numbers.

  • Explains why elements in the same group exhibit similar chemical behavior
  • Supports the rationale for the structure of the modern periodic table
  • Forms the foundation for predicting element properties and reactions

Periodic Trends and Applications

Periodic trends help predict atomic behavior, reactivity, and chemical bonding:

  • Atomic radius decreases across periods, increases down groups
  • Ionization energy increases across periods, decreases down groups
  • Electronegativity varies predictably and affects bonding types
  • Useful in predicting chemical reactions, material properties, and electron configurations
