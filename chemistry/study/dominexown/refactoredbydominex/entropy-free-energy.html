Entropy and Free Energy

Entropy (S)

Entropy is a measure of the disorder or randomness of a system. Higher entropy means more disorder.

For a reversible process:

dS = δQ_rev / T

where δQ_rev is heat absorbed reversibly and T is temperature in Kelvin.

Gibbs Free Energy (G)

Gibbs free energy determines the spontaneity of a process at constant temperature and pressure:

G = H - T S

Change in free energy:

ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

  • ΔG < 0: spontaneous process
  • ΔG > 0: non-spontaneous process
  • ΔG = 0: equilibrium

Applications

  • Predicting whether chemical reactions occur spontaneously
  • Calculating maximum work obtainable from reactions
  • Understanding phase transitions and equilibrium processes
