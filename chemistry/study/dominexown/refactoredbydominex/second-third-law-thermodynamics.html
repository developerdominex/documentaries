Second and Third Laws of Thermodynamics

Second Law

The second law states that the entropy of an isolated system always increases over time. It implies that natural processes are irreversible.

Third Law

The third law states that the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero (0 K).

Applications

  • Predicting spontaneity of reactions
  • Understanding heat engines and refrigerators
  • Calculating absolute entropies of substances
