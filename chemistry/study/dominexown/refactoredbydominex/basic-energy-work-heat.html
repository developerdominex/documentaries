Basic Concepts: Energy, Work, and Heat

Introduction

Thermodynamics deals with energy and its transformations. Key concepts include energy, work, and heat.

Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work. Forms include:

  • Kinetic energy: motion of particles
  • Potential energy: stored energy due to position
  • Chemical energy: stored in chemical bonds

Work

Work is done when a force is applied over a distance:

W = F × d

In thermodynamics, work is often related to expansion or compression of gases:

W = P × ΔV

Heat

Heat is energy transferred due to a temperature difference. Measured in joules (J) or calories (cal).
