Definition

Metallic bonding is the force of attraction between delocalized valence electrons and positively charged metal ions in a lattice.

Electron Sea Model

Valence electrons are free to move throughout the metal lattice, forming a "sea of electrons" that accounts for conductivity and malleability.

Properties

  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Malleable and ductile
  • Shiny luster
  • High melting and boiling points

Examples

  • Sodium (Na)
  • Copper (Cu)
  • Iron (Fe)
