Liquids and Their Properties

Introduction

Liquids have definite volume but take the shape of their container. They exhibit intermolecular forces that define their properties.

Intermolecular Forces

  • Hydrogen bonding
  • Dipole-dipole interactions
  • London dispersion forces

Surface Tension

Energy required to increase the surface area of a liquid. Caused by cohesive forces between molecules.

Viscosity and Flow

Resistance to flow, depends on temperature and intermolecular forces.

Capillary Action

Rise or fall of liquid in narrow tubes due to cohesion and adhesion forces.

Evaporation and Boiling

Evaporation occurs at all temperatures, while boiling occurs at a specific temperature when vapor pressure equals atmospheric pressure.
